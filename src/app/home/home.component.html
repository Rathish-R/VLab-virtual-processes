    • About the Page

    Virtual processes in chemical engineering can be used to perform a variety of calculations and simulations to model the behaviour of chemical processes and systems. For example, users can perform calculations related to reaction kinetics, thermodynamics, and mass and energy balances to gain insights into the behaviour of chemical reactions and processes. They can also simulate transport phenomena such as diffusion, convection, and heat transfer to understand the movement of materials and heat in a system. Additionally, virtual process equipments can be used to model the behaviour of chemical processes under different conditions, such as variations in temperature, pressure, and composition, allowing users to gain a deeper understanding of how these factors affect the performance of a chemical system. By providing a flexible and interactive platform for these types of calculations and simulations, virtual process equipments can help to advance the field of chemical engineering and provide valuable insights into the behaviour of chemical systems.
