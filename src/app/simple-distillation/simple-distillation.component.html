Theory

The simplest form of butch still consists of a heated vessel (pot or boiler), a condenser. and one or more receiving tanks. No trays or packing are provided. Feed is charged into the vessel and brought to boiling. Vapors are condensed and collected in a receiver No reflux is returned. The rate of vaporization is sometimes controlled to prevent "bumping the charge and to avoid overloading the condenser, but other controls are minimal. This process is often referred to as Rayleigh distillation.
